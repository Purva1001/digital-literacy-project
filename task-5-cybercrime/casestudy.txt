CASE STUDY: UPI/ONLINE PAYMENT FRAUD


What is it?
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of 
cybercrime where fraudsters trick users into sending 
money or revealing sensitive information through 
deceptive means.

How it typically happens (step-by-step):
1. The victim receives a call/message from someone 
   claiming to be a bank official or customer support
2. The fraudster says there is a problem with the 
   victim's account or offers a cashback/reward
3. The victim is asked to share their UPI PIN, OTP, 
   or to scan a QR code
4. Once the victim shares the information or scans 
   the QR code, money is instantly debited from 
   their account
5. The fraudster disconnects and becomes unreachable

Who is usually targeted?
- First-time UPI users
- College students and young adults
- Elderly people unfamiliar with digital payments
- People who have recently posted "item for sale" 
  on platforms like OLX or Facebook Marketplace

Consequences:
- Immediate financial loss
- Mental stress and anxiety
- Loss of trust in digital payment systems
- Difficulty recovering money as transactions 
  are instant and often irreversible
- In severe cases, entire savings can be wiped out

Source: National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)
