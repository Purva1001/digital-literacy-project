STAY SAFE ONLINE — PREVENTION CHECKLIST FOR COLLEGE STUDENTS IN INDIA


1. Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or password 
   with anyone — not even bank officials

2. Never scan a QR code sent by a stranger — 
   scanning a QR code can SEND money, not receive it

3. Always verify the identity of the caller before 
   sharing any information — call back on the 
   official bank number to confirm

4. Use strong, unique passwords for all your 
   accounts and enable two-factor authentication

5. Do not click on suspicious links in SMS, 
   WhatsApp, or email — these may be phishing attempts

6. Always download apps from official sources only 
   (Google Play Store or Apple App Store)

7. Regularly check your bank statements and UPI 
   transaction history for any unauthorized activity

8. Never use public Wi-Fi for banking or UPI 
   transactions

FINANCIAL/UPI SPECIFIC TIPS:
- Your bank will NEVER ask for your UPI PIN or OTP
- "Collect request" on UPI means money will be 
  TAKEN from you — never approve unknown requests

REPORTING:
- National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in
- Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (24x7)
- Report immediately — the sooner you report, 
  the better the chance of recovering money
